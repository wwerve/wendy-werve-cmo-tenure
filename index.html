01 — Two years, in the round

Marketing as the engine that delivered.

Across 24 months, the marketing function moved from a fragmented brand and an underperforming demand engine to delivering above plan in FY25 — and driving the company's best-ever bookings quarter (Q4 2025) and best-ever Q1 (Q1 2026).

The open question through FY25 was whether the pipeline would convert. Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 answered it back-to-back: Q4 2025 closed at 117% bookings attainment to deliver the company's best-ever bookings quarter, and Q1 2026 followed with the best-ever Q1 in company history at 103% of plan, +7% YoY. Marketing-attributed bookings hit 109% of plan, +40% YoY — and Marketing's share of total ARR bookings climbed from 37% to 48.5% over the year. The funnel was converting more efficiently than at any point in CMO tenure.

The team was built deliberately, hire by hire — operating leverage from disciplined scaling. With the exception of one team member who pre-dated CMO arrival, every member of the marketing organization was a CMO-tenure recruit. Hiring was sequenced against business need, not headcount budget — each role added to the team mapped to a specific gap in the funnel, the brand, or the global footprint. As the team grew, pipeline grew faster: output per marketer increased even as headcount expanded. The team delivered through one acquisition, three rebrands, the buildout of a UK presence, the opening of an India office, and the company's best-ever Q1 in history.
