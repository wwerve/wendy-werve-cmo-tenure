01 — Two years, in the round

Marketing as the engine that delivered.

Across 24 months, the marketing function moved from a fragmented brand and an underperforming demand engine to the only revenue function in the business to exceed plan in FY25 — and the engine driving the company's best-ever bookings quarter (Q4 2025) and best-ever Q1 (Q1 2026).

The open question through FY25 was whether the pipeline would convert. Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 answered it back-to-back: Q4 2025 closed at 117% bookings attainment to deliver the company's best-ever bookings quarter, and Q1 2026 followed with the best-ever Q1 in company history at 103% of plan, +7% YoY. Marketing-attributed bookings hit 109% of plan, +40% YoY — and Marketing's share of total ARR bookings climbed from 37% to 48.5% over the year. The funnel was converting more efficiently than at any point in CMO tenure.

04 — Operating discipline

Modernized the engine. Made the structural calls.

Marketing modernized its operating infrastructure across attribution, martech, AI capability, and structural alignment with revenue functions.

Attribution & performance
Built first-time paid media attribution, achieving 8.1× pipeline ROAS against a 3× target. Established Salesforce attribution rigor across every event in the portfolio. Improved program spend efficiency by 23% year-over-year (pipeline-per-dollar-of-program-spend, FY24 → FY25). Personally scored 13 enterprise sales calls against a custom MEDDIC-style rubric to diagnose conversion gaps and inform enablement design.
Martech modernization
Modernized the martech stack — implemented Qualified (AI SDR + speed-to-lead), Profound and AEO (search), 6sense (intent + ABM), and Upside (GTM intelligence + attribution). Replaced spreadsheet-driven attribution with platform-driven decisioning across the funnel.
AI capability
Designed and launched team-wide AI training in 2024 — ahead of the curve for B2B marketing teams. The team has been operating with AI fluency across content, analytics, and campaign work for two years, with agents now scaling across all marketing functions.
Structural alignment
Brought the SDR organization under Marketing in Q1 2026. Made the December 2025 call to merge the SDR function with Marketing effective January 1, 2026, tightening lead-handoff alignment and feedback loops. The channel responded immediately, moving from FY25 underperformance to 111% of pipeline plan by the end of Q1 2026 — a 58-point swing in attainment in a single quarter. Operating leverage from a deliberate going-into-the-year structural call, paid out within the quarter it took effect.
